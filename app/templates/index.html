Phishing is a big problem

Every day, non technical users fall victim to online phishing scams because they don't have the technical skill required to authenticate clients. People often only rely on spotting typos to detect impersonation.

In the 21st Century, that isn't enough.

The advice given in many training packages is based on spotting standard signs like checking for poor spelling and grammar, and while these can be a good place to start, they can’t be used to spot all phishing emails. Bad guys can spell (and some nice genuine people can’t)

Kate R, NCSC 2018

Here's our solution

Checkmate allows users to mutually authenticate clients in their communications, from emails to calls, using a secret code only the client can access. This allows users to detect scammers who can't access their secret code.
